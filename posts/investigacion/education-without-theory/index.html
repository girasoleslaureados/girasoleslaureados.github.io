Education without theory

Saturday, Feb, 2021 By chriss-lauj.

The debate that has absorved great deal of intellectual energy is about what is educational theory, what is it for and what it tries to achieve.

Up to the end of the 19th century educational did not exist despite the canonical texts as Plato’s Republic and Rousseau’s Emile.

Social progress depended on making our practices and institutions less dependent on custom, habit, dogma and tradition and more firmly based on knowledge that met universal standars of objetivity, this knowledge is called theory.

Teacher were trained acording to the pupil-teacher method. an apprenticeship system in which novice teachers learned how to teach under the supervision of an experted teacher. Learning a mixture of tradition, maxims, dogma and rules

What is the epistemological basis of educational theory? How does educational theory relate to educational practice?

Philosophy, psychology, sociology and history are the foundation disciplines and educational theory is only the appplication to education of these four.

The assumptions from the 20th century debate allow aspects to become more clear like theory is everything that practice is not, educational theory is abstract rather than concrete, general rather than particular, context-free rather than context-dependent. Educational theory always stands apart form the practice it aspires to affect.

Educational theory can only achieve its practical purpore if the pivotal role of philosophy as preserved.

Foundationalist refer to the general belief that the only way we can justify our beliefs is to show how they rest on some basic beliefs or foundatoins that do not stand in need of justification

Postfoundationalist

Educational theory cannot inform practice because itself is a form of practice. The theoretical generalisatoins are always abstractions from the malleable world of practice and thus always gonna be shaped by the by the very features of practice.

There are no unmediated facts and no neutral observations. Facts are not the sort of things one observes independently of a particular way of describing the world… What we observe depends on the concepts and beliefs we bring to these observations - PRING author of the Philosophy and Educational research -

What practicioners are commited to is not a theory but a ser of beliefs which provide them with their interpretative understanding of THEIR practice, they are not engaging to a general theoyr.

No practice stands outside a theorical framework, that is a framework of interconnected beliefs
